# Portfolio Inspiration

## Reference Portfolios
> Add links and notes on portfolios you love and why

| Portfolio | Link | What I love about it |
|-----------|------|----------------------|
| Tamar Segura | https://www.tamarsegura.com/ | Really clean and aesthetic. Exactly the right amount of info to tell a good story. Honest, visually strong storytelling. Case studies have a clear narrative arc (problem → iterations → solution → impact) and she's transparent about what didn't work. User quotes overlaid on UI screenshots ground decisions in real feedback. Annotated screens that explain the "why." Closes with hard KPIs and external validation. Minimal layout with generous whitespace — complex B2B product stays readable. No fluff. |

| Pendar Yousefi | https://mahimoto.com/ | Excellent case study structure: clear metadata up top (role, company, timeline, team), then a narrative arc that's honest about what failed before showing what worked. Data-driven storytelling — backs up decisions with real numbers (e.g. "40% didn't know the feature existed", "25% increase in usage"). Shows the full design thinking process, not just polished finals. Annotated screenshots that explain the "why" behind UI choices. Minimal layout with lots of whitespace — lets the work breathe. |
