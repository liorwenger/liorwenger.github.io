Associate Network Dashboard

B2B SaaS Platform | Legal Tech

iPeer OS helps law firms manage global partner relationships across 170+ jurisdictions. The platform served paralegals well - but the people who decide whether to adopt it had no visibility into network performance or ROI.

I designed the Associate Network Dashboard to bridge that gap - giving managing partners the strategic intelligence they need to evaluate, optimize, and justify their associate network.

The Problem

The user we didn't design for

We designed for the people who use the system daily. Then we expanded our focus to serve the people who decide whether to adopt it. Different users, different needs - both essential.

Sarah - The Paralegal

Our primary user

Paralegals manage foreign filing workflows, coordinate with global associates, and track deadlines. They're detail-oriented, efficiency-driven, and judged on flawless execution.

"I need to file this patent in 12 countries by Friday. iPeer helps me get competitive quotes and execute fast."

Michael - Managing Partner

The decision maker we missed

What he needs: A way to walk into a quarterly review and know - without digging - whether the firm's associate relationships are healthy, balanced, and worth renewing. Right now he gets a spreadsheet once a quarter. By the time it reaches him, it's already outdated. He's making six-figure relationship decisions on stale data and gut feeling.

"Are we working with the right partners? How do I prove this matters?"

Where We Started

A dashboard built for tasks, not decisions

The existing dashboard gave managing partners data - but no direction. In practice, most partners ignored it entirely. Those who did open it would scan the associate list, find nothing obviously wrong, and close the tab. There was no way to tell if 66h response time was a problem or normal. No way to see if the firm was sending far more work to Tokyo than it was receiving back. The data was there. The meaning wasn't.

No performance signals

No way to tell which associates are reliable and which are falling behind on deadlines or quality.

No reciprocity visibility

Firms couldn't see if the work exchange with partners was balanced or one-sided.

No actionable insights

Data was present but passive. Nothing told users what needed attention or why.

Design Strategy

Three principles that guided every decision

01

Actionable over informational

Every metric should answer "so what?" - not just show a number, but tell the user what it means and what to do about it.

02

Network-level view first

Start with the big picture health of the entire network, then let users drill into individual associates. Strategic before operational.

03

Surface what's urgent

Don't make partners hunt for problems. Proactively flag deadlines at risk, imbalanced relationships, and declining performance.

Design Process

From data dump to strategic tool

MVP - The data is there. Now what?

We organized everything into structured widgets - metrics, coverage, performance, and reciprocity. It was comprehensive, but when partners saw it, they all had the same reaction: "I don't know what needs my attention."

!

Feedback from stakeholders. "I can see the data, but I don't know what needs my attention." The dashboard showed everything equally - nothing stood out as urgent or important.

Iteration 1 - Urgency Banner

In usability testing, we got feedback that although partners aren't the ones dealing with deadlines (that's the paralegals), they still want to know immediately if there's a tight deadline approaching.

!

What we added. An emergency banner at the top of the dashboard that shows cases with a deadline coming soon - so partners can spot urgency at a glance.

Iteration 2 - When numbers need context, not color

Users couldn't tell if the numbers were good or bad - the bars looked similar at a glance, the colors had lost meaning from overuse, and nothing showed whether things were improving or declining. We replaced the chart with three numbers, each with a trend and a plain-language verdict.

!

What we changed. Replaced the color-coded bar chart with three clear metrics - each showing the number, its 6-month trend, and a written verdict like "Declining - review associates" so partners know exactly what needs attention.

Iteration 3 - Don't make me think. Tell me who to act on.

The scatter plot failed before users even tried to read it - partners simply didn't recognize it as a tool for their work. They come from a legal world, not a data world. A chart with axes and bubble sizes asked them to learn a new visual language just to answer one question: which associates need my attention? We replaced it with a priority list that speaks their language. Names, scores, and one clear action per associate.

!

What we changed. Replaced the scatter plot with a ranked associate list - each row shows the associate's name, performance score, and a single recommended action like "Find alternatives" or "Send 3 more referrals."

The Result

The final dashboard

A strategic command center that gives managing partners the visibility they need to evaluate, optimize, and justify their global associate network.

Key Design Decisions

Urgency Banner

Cases approaching deadlines surface at the very top. No scrolling needed - the most critical items are impossible to miss.

Performance Alerts

Instead of just showing "75% on-time," we added trend indicators and a written alert explaining what's declining and a suggested next step.

Agreement-Based Reciprocity

Moved from raw sent/received counts to tracking against actual agreements. "On track" vs "Behind" is more useful than "sent 3 more than received."

Priority Associate List

Associates who need action surface first with specific recommendations - "Find alternatives," "Consider replacing," "Send 3 more" - not just a red status dot.
